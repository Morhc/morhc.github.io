BOOK RECOMMENDATIONS

I really enjoy reading. Probably my only hobby. My reading habits are mostly informed by my desire to live in a more equitable world and learning what I can do to contribute to that. My understanding of what that means is filtered through a Christian lens that is Presbyterian in orientation, Anglican liturgically, and has an eye towards liberation theology. God is the God of the oppressed, and as a Christian I feel a duty to care for the vulnerable in my local context as well as I can.

If you want to stay up to date with what I'm reading check out my Goodreads.

God-talk:

  • The First Canonical Gospel of Christ ascribed to St. Mark the Evangelist in the NRSVue translation
  • A Theology of Liberation by Gustavo Gutiérrez
  • The Presence of the Kingdom by Jacques Ellul
  • God Here and Now by Karl Barth
  • Dogmatics in Outline by Karl Barth
  • The Wisdom of the Desert as collected by Thomas Merton
  • The Shaking of the Foundations a collection of Paul Tillich
  • New Testament & Mythology: And Other Basic Writings a collection of Rudolf Bultmann
  • Tradition and Apocalypse: An Essay on the Future of Christian Belief by David Bentley Hart
  • Ponder These Things: Praying With Icons of the Virign by Rowan Williams
  • Our Mother Earth: A Christian Reading of the Challenge of the Environment a collection of Pope Francis

Social analysis:

  • Women, Race, and Class by Angela Davis
  • How to be an Antiracist by Ibram X. Kendi
  • Simulacra and Simulation by Jean Baudrillard
  • All About Love by bell hooks
  • The Will to Change by bell hooks
  • The Xenofeminist Manifesto by Laboria Cuboniks
  • The Force of Nonviolence: The Ethical in the Political by Judith Butler

Other thoughts:

  • In the Arms of the Beloved a collection of Rumi
  • Life of Galileo by Bertolt Brecht
  • Ender's Game by Orson Scott Card
  • The Warded Man by Peter Brett

If you want a copy of any of these books, I would ask that you don't order from Amazon because they are evil [see here]. Almost all books can be found at the library, locally run bookstores, secondhand, online, or through the publisher's website.
