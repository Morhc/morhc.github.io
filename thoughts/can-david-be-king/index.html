Can David Be King?

Posted on June 18, 2026

There are many layers of tradition in the text of the Hebrew Bible that make evident that it is not univocal. One such case that I stumbled on yesterday listening to a live stream from Kipp Davis is that according to the Deuteronomist, the whole of the Davidic line cannot legitimately enter the assembly of YHWH.

Deuteronomy 23.3-6 “No Ammonite or Moabite shall come into the assembly of the Lord even to the tenth generation. None of their descendants shall come into the assembly of the Lord forever, because they did not meet you with food and water on your journey out of Egypt and because they hired against you Balaam son of Beor, from Pethor of Mesopotamia, to curse you. (Yet the Lord your God refused to heed Balaam; the Lord your God turned the curse into a blessing for you, because the Lord your God loved you.) You shall never promote their welfare or their prosperity as long as you live.
Ruth 1.1-5; 4.13, 17 In the days when the judges ruled, there was a famine in the land, and a certain man of Bethlehem in Judah went to live in the country of Moab, he and his wife and two sons. The name of the man was Elimelech and the name of his wife Naomi, and the names of his two sons were Mahlon and Chilion; they were Ephrathites from Bethlehem in Judah. They went into the country of Moab and remained there. But Elimelech, the husband of Naomi, died, and she was left with her two sons. These took Moabite wives; the name of the one was Orpah and the name of the other Ruth. When they had lived there about ten years, both Mahlon and Chilion also died, so that the woman was left without her two sons and her husband.
So Boaz took Ruth, and she became his wife. When they came together, the Lord made her conceive, and she bore a son... The women of the neighborhood gave him a name, saying, “A son has been born to Naomi.” They named him Obed; he became the father of Jesse, the father of David.

It's pretty cut and dry if you make these univocal. Because David is (matrilineally!) a descendent of Ruth the Moabite, he cannot enter the assembly of YHWH. Neither can the builder of the temple Solomon enter. Can the Davidic line even sit on the throne? Israel is commanded that they never shall promote the welfare and prosperity of Ammonites and Moabites along with their descendents. And, since the prohibition applies to their descendents "even to the tenth generation" and "forever", one has to ask the question. Jesus is something like 30 generations down the line from Ruth. So, reading these texts univocally and inerrantly, is Jesus in the clear (to the tenth, literally) or not (forever excluded). Can He enter into the assembly of YHWH?

Apparently there is a work-around in the halakha (because of course there is) to solve this particular issue.

Yevamot 76b Ammonite and Moabite converts are prohibited from entering into the congregation and marrying a woman who was born Jewish, and their prohibition is eternal, for all generations. However, their female counterparts, even the convert herself, are permitted immediately.

מַתְנִי׳ עַמּוֹנִי וּמוֹאָבִי אֲסוּרִים וְאִיסּוּרָן אִיסּוּר עוֹלָם אֲבָל נְקֵבוֹתֵיהֶם מוּתָּרוֹת מִיָּד

The dialogue surrounding this conclusion of the Rabbis is really interesting actually and relates to the double-meeting of Saul and David as well. Some imagined conversation between Saul, Do'eg the Edomite, and Abner about David descending from Ruth. Certainly would recommend a read. It's really fun to read about Saul, Do'eg, and Abner argue the legal legitimacy of David's claim to kingship on the battlefield about to encounter Goliath. The argument rests on the idea that the word in the previous verse talking of "one of illegitimate birth" (מַמְזֵ֖ר) is a masculine word and therefore subsequent referrents must also be about masculine converts not feminine, therefore Ruth and her descendents are legitimately able to enter the assembly and David can be king (and Solomon can build the temple) (and Jesus can be the Messiah). Whether this argument is convincing is another story altogether. I think that it's just easier to say that these stitched layers just don't cohere and don't fret about it.
